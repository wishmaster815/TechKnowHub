Mini 20 Liter Solar Water Heater for Rooftop

A Mini 20 Liter Solar Water Heater is designed to provide a small amount of hot water using solar energy. It's a sustainable and cost-effective solution for residential use, particularly in regions with abundant sunlight.

How Does this project work

1. Solar Collector: The heart of the system is a solar collector, often mounted on the rooftop. It consists of a series of solar panels or tubes designed to absorb solar radiation.

2. Heat Absorption: Solar collectors are typically made of materials that efficiently absorb solar radiation and convert it into heat. Common collector types include flat-plate collectors and evacuated tube collectors.

3. Heat Transfer: A heat transfer fluid, often a glycol-water mixture, flows through the collector's absorber, absorbing heat from the sun.

4. Heat Exchange: The heated fluid is then pumped through a heat exchanger or coil in a water storage tank. This transfers the heat from the fluid to the water in the tank.

5. Thermal Storage: The hot water is stored in an insulated tank, usually with a capacity of around 20 liters. This tank may have additional heating elements for backup or cloudy days.

6. Temperature Control: To ensure a constant and safe water temperature, a temperature controller is used to regulate the operation of the solar collector and backup heating elements.

7. Hot Water Distribution: The hot water can be connected to your existing plumbing system, providing hot water on demand for various household applications like showers, sinks, or dishwashers.

Building the Project

1. Design and Planning: Determine the size, type, and location of the solar collector, as well as the capacity of the water storage tank. Consider factors like sunlight availability and local climate.

2. Solar Collector Installation: Mount the solar collector on the rooftop at the optimal angle and orientation to maximize solar exposure. Ensure it is securely fastened and connected to the heat transfer fluid circuit.

3. Water Storage Tank: Install the insulated water storage tank in a suitable location on the rooftop. Connect it to the heat exchanger or coil from the collector.

4. Piping and Circulation: Install piping and circulation pumps to move the heat transfer fluid between the collector and the tank. Ensure proper insulation to minimize heat loss.

5. Temperature Control: Set up a temperature controller to monitor and control the water temperature. This controller can activate the circulation pump and backup heating elements as needed.

6. Backup Heating Elements: Install electric heating elements in the tank as a backup for cloudy days or high demand. These elements can be controlled by the temperature controller.

7. Testing and Calibration: Test the system to ensure it's operating efficiently and providing hot water at the desired temperature. Calibrate the temperature controller as needed.

8. Monitoring and Maintenance: Regularly monitor the system's performance and perform routine maintenance, such as cleaning the collector and checking for leaks.

9. Usage and Optimization: Educate users on the system's operation and optimize its performance by adjusting settings based on seasonal variations in sunlight.

Things You'll Need

  • Solar Collector
  • Storage Tank
  • Circulation Pump
  • Heat Exchanger
  • Temperature and Pressure Relief Valve
  • Control System
